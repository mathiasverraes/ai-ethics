# AI Ethics

To create awareness, let's collect ethical issues caused by AI and AI adoption. 


## Contribute

- Ask mathias@verraes.net / @mathiasverraes for write permissions.
- The current goal is to create a document for each issue, with a problem description and possible remedies.
- Annotate with peer reviewed science. Copy the documents into the repo if allowed.
- Use AI thoughtfully if at all. Whether true or not, assume that AI is compromised and will not act against its owners' interests.
- For now, pull requests will only be filtered to keep out obvious junk, so don't assume content in the main branch is reviewed. Submit more PRs to correct.
- All tooling should be written in Typescript unless that's not possible.
- Scout rule: leave this repo better than you found it (correctness, scientific evidencen, repo organisation, templates, tools, writing quality, ...)

The current rules can be changed through PRs or debate. 

## Issues

Here's a starting list of issue, off the top my head:

- billionaires investing in AI to control public opinion (already happening with GrokAI)
- Energy consumption, use of fossils, climate change
- water use, impacting local communities
- land use for data centers, low frequency noise and other impacts on locals
- mass layoffs
- colonialist exploitation of people in developing countries for training LLMs 
- the training involves looking classifying images and descriptions of violence and other horrible things, as well as writing such material to train the filters. All with no psychological support and at low pay
- Humans favour sycophantic answers, and LLMs are trained to be sycophantic so customers don't switch to a competitor. This results in echo chambers and misinformation
- there are already studies indicating lower cognitive skills and critical thinking in heavy users 
- massive investments required, without evidence that these companies can break even
- 30% of the stock market is in 8 AI companies
- they are all interconnected (eg MSFT invest in OpenAI to spend on MSFT compute)
- risk of economic collapse, or some of the fish getting eaten by bigger fish, power concentration
- advertising can be a lot a more subtle: llms recommending sponsors' products, or recommending solutions where that product is the best option 
- bias in training data (racist, gendered, colonialist, class ...)
- use of AI in surveillance
- use of AI in warfare
- fake content, disinformation 
- techno-optimism: the belief that all the world's problems will be solved by tech, and that therefore we don't need to worry about any of these issues
- replacing entry-level roles might cause lack of experts in the future
- training based on stolen & copyrighted data
- lack of oversight in training data
- dead internet theory
- mass-produced slop replacing journalism, arts, literature, music
- original content can't compete with slop
- AI training on AI slop could cause degeneration of models
- AI becoming expensive can widen the wealth gap
- mission critical or life critical systems built with insufficient human oversight, causing potential disasters
- "a broken clock is right twice a day": No, a broken clock is always wrong, as the only way to verify it is using a working clock. Occasional hallucination is more dangerous than assumed
- Gell-Mann Amnesia Effect
- lack of accountability ("The AI did it!")
- human workers being instructed by AI to do the work AI can't
- workers are alienated from the product of their labour (cfr Marx, but for white collar work)

Issues I'm personally not worried about at this time, but open to be proven wrong:

- AGI
- artificial consciousness
- AI enslaving humans
- religious concerns, role of humans and their relationship to God/gods
- AI brain implants
